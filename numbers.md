# **Sierpiński–Conway Lambda Notation – Naming System**

This document describes the naming rules used for numbers expressed in the **Sierpiński–Conway Lambda Notation (SCLN)**.  
It defines a linguistic structure based on **prefixes** (which depend on the lambda level) and **suffixes** (which depend on the notational class).

---

## **1. Lambda Numbers**

Numbers expressed under the **basic lambda notation** receive the suffix **-itol**.

Each lambda level uses a specific prefix that indicates how many times the lambda operation is iterated:

| Lambda Level | Prefix | Example Name | Notation | Description |
|:--------------|:--------|:--------------|:-----------|:-------------|
| λ₁(n) | Mono- | **Monoitol** | λ₁(n) | The most basic lambda number; roughly exponential in scale. |
| λ₂(n) | Diar- | **Diaritol** | λ₂(n) | Comparable to tetration levels. |
| λ₃(n) | Triar- | **Triaritol** | λ₃(n) | Represents pentation-like magnitude. |
| λ₄(n) | Carti- | **Cartiitol** | λ₄(n) | Equivalent to a hexation-scale function. |
| λ₅(n) | Pentum- | **Pentumitol** | λ₅(n) | Corresponds to higher hyperoperations beyond hexation. |
| λ₆(n) | Sictum- | **Sictumitol** | λ₆(n) | Grows at roughly the 7th hyperoperation level. |
| λ₇(n) | Septil- | **Septilitol** | λ₇(n) | Represents a self-escalating growth layer. |
| λ₈(n) | Hocta- | **Hoctaitol** | λ₈(n) | Expands faster than most conventional hyperoperations. |
| λ₉(n) | Ginona- | **Ginonaitol** | λ₉(n) | Represents extremely high lambda iteration depth. |
| λₙ(n), n ≥ 10 | Met- | **Metitol** | λₙ(n) | Used for any lambda level beyond λ₉. |

---

## **2. Omega Numbers**

Numbers expressed under the **basic omega notation** follow the same prefix pattern,  
but instead of the suffix **-itol**, they use the suffix **-megus**.

| Omega Level | Prefix | Example Name | Notation | Description |
|:--------------|:--------|:--------------|:-----------|:-------------|
| ω₁(n) | Mono- | **Monomegus** | ω₁(n) | The first omega number, extending λ-operations. |
| ω₂(n) | Diar- | **Diarmegus** | ω₂(n) | Two layers of omega-iteration. |
| ω₃(n) | Triar- | **Triarmegus** | ω₃(n) | Three layers of omega-iteration. |
| ω₄(n) | Carti- | **Cartimegus** | ω₄(n) | A higher recursive omega form. |
| ω₅(n) | Pentum- | **Pentummegus** | ω₅(n) | Fifth-level recursive omega. |
| ω₆(n) | Sictum- | **Sictummegus** | ω₆(n) | Represents transfinite-like growth. |
| ω₇(n) | Septil- | **Septilmegus** | ω₇(n) | Rapid escalation beyond finite meta-iteration. |
| ω₈(n) | Hocta- | **Hoctamegus** | ω₈(n) | Deep ω-level hierarchy. |
| ω₉(n) | Ginona- | **Ginonamegus** | ω₉(n) | Expands through massive recursive depth. |
| ωₙ(n), n ≥ 10 | Met- | **Metamegus** | ωₙ(n) | Meta-omega forms, approaching transordinal rates. |

---

## **3. Alphabetic Escalation Numbers**

Numbers defined through the **alphabetic hierarchy** (such as α, β, γ, δ, …)  
follow the same prefix system again, but use the suffix **-exus**.

| Alphabetic Level | Prefix | Example Name | Notation | Description |
|:------------------|:--------|:--------------|:-----------|:-------------|
| α₁(n) | Mono- | **Monoexus** | α₁(n) | The first alphabetic escalation beyond ω. |
| α₂(n) | Diar- | **Diarexus** | α₂(n) | Double-layered alphabetic iteration. |
| α₃(n) | Triar- | **Triarexus** | α₃(n) | Extends well beyond omega hierarchies. |
| α₄(n) | Carti- | **Cartiexus** | α₄(n) | Fourth escalation, on transordinal scale. |
| α₅(n) | Pentum- | **Pentumexus** | α₅(n) | Fifth escalation level. |
| α₆(n) | Sictum- | **Sictumexus** | α₆(n) | Recursive self-iterative alphabetic layer. |
| α₇(n) | Septil- | **Septilexus** | α₇(n) | Meta-alphabetic expansion beyond ε₀. |
| α₈(n) | Hocta- | **Hoctaexus** | α₈(n) | Deep meta-level of iteration. |
| α₉(n) | Ginona- | **Ginonexus** | α₉(n) | Gigantic function at the edge of transfinite recursion. |
| αₙ(n), n ≥ 10 | Met- | **Metexus** | αₙ(n) | Generalized escalation beyond the Γ₀ level. |

---

## **4. Summary**

- **Suffixes define the notational class:**
  - **–itol** → Basic Lambda Notation  
  - **–megus** → Omega-Level Notation  
  - **–exus** → Alphabetic Escalation Notation  

- **Prefixes define the iteration depth** of the operation.  
  They can combine with suffixes to denote function magnitude or structural order.

### **Example Names**

| Expression | Name | Description |
|:------------|:------|:-------------|
| λ₃(10) | **Triaritol** | The third lambda number with base 10. |
| ω₄(10,2) | **Cartimegus** | Omega-notation version of λ₄, double-iterated. |
| α₆(3,2,5) | **Sictumexus** | Sixth-level alphabetic escalation; meta-iterated five times. |
| λ₁₀(10,100) | **Metitol** | Ten-level lambda tower with base 10. |
| ω₁₀(10) | **Metamegus** | Ten-level omega meta-operator. |
| Ω_β(10,100) | **Metexus** | Alphabetic extension at high transordinal depth. |

---

### **Notes**

- The prefixes are **phonetic and symbolic**, not numeric.  
  They serve as linguistic identifiers for iteration depth in SCLN.  
- The **suffix system** unifies the hierarchy across lambda, omega, and alphabetic forms.  
- These names can be freely extended using new Greek letters or combined forms  
  for higher or mixed meta-notations.
